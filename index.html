Bertha Lutz

Brazilian zoologist, feminist, politician and diplomat.

Bertha Lutz laughing at an ONU meeting, surrounded by men

Bertha Lutz biggest achievements

  • 1894 - Born in São Paulo, Brasil

  • 1918 - Graduate in Natural Sciences, biology and zoology at the University of Paris - Sorbonne

  • 1919 - A year after returning to Brazil, Lutz was hired by the Museu Nacional do Rio de Janeiro and founded the League for Intellectual Emancipation of Women and was appointed to represent the Brazilian government in the Female International Council of the International Labour Organization (ILO).

  • 1922 - created the Brazilian Federation for Women's Progress (1922), a political group which advocated for Brazilian women's rights, most importantly, their right to vote. Also, served as a delegate to the Pan-American Conference of Women in Baltimore, Maryland.

  • 1925 - she was elected president of the Inter-American Union of Women.

  • 1931 - Brazilian women finally gained the right to vote.

  • 1933 - after obtaining her law degree from Federal University of Rio de Janeiro Faculty of Law, Lutz participated and introduced several proposals for gender equity in the Inter-American Conference of Montevideo, Uruguay.

  • 1935 - Lutz decided to run for National Congress of Brazil and came in second behind Cándido Pessoa, and replaced him when he died a year later, making Lutz one of the few Brazilian Congresswomen of the time.

  • 1943 - Publishes “Observations on the life history of the Brazilian Frog” 

  • 1945 - She was one of the four women to sign the United Nations Charter at the Inter-American Conference of Women held in San Francisco.

  • 1946 - Publishes  “A notable frog chorus in Brazil” 

  • 1952 - Publishes “New frogs from Itatiaia mountain”

  • 1953 to 1959 - served as vice president of the Inter-American Commission of Women

  • 1958 - she described what is now known as Lutz's rapids frog (Paratelmatobius lutzii Lutz and Carvalho, 1958), which is named in honor of her father.

  • 1964 - Lutz headed the Brazilian delegation at the 14th Inter-American Commission in Montevideo

  • 1970 - at the 15th annual meeting of the Inter-American Commission of Women, she proposed to hold a seminar dedicated to addressing the specific problems faced by indigenous women.

  • 1975 - Although she was a little over seventy during this stage of her life, Lutz continued to attend conferences and push for the expansion of women's rights, including the World Conference on Women, in Mexico City.

  • 1976 - Died at age of 82
